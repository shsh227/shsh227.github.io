New eye drops could replace reading glasses

VIZZ is the first aceclidine-based eye drop solution designed to relieve age-related farsightedness. The drops slightly constrict the pupil, allowing up to ten hours of clear vision – without reading glasses. Clinical studies confirm good tolerability. VIZZ is expected to be available in the U.S. by the end of 2025.

Record low: Fewer americans are drinking alcohol

Alcohol Consumption Hits Historic Low in the U.S.. In the U.S., alcohol consumption is falling to a historic low. According to a Gallup survey, only 54 percent of Americans report drinking alcohol – the lowest level since the end of Prohibition in 1939.

Crime in the U.S. declines in 2024

Violent crime in the U.S. fell by 4.5% in 2024, including a nearly 15% drop in homicides, according to an FBI report. Property crimes also decreased: vehicle thefts by 18.6%, burglaries by 8.6%, and larcenies by 5.5%. This continues the downward trend in crime observed since the COVID-19 pandemic.

U.S.: Solar Panels for Balconies and Patios on the Rise

Small solar systems are gaining popularity in the U.S. as tax incentives for rooftop installations are phased out. They are affordable, easy to install, and help reduce CO₂ emissions. For many homeowners, these compact systems could be an attractive alternative.

MeluXina: Luxembourg’s Green Supercomputer

Our modern world relies on supercomputers – which consume a lot of CO₂. MeluXina demonstrates a more environmentally friendly approach: the supercomputer is powered entirely by renewable energy, primarily from Norwegian hydropower, and cleverly uses waste heat for cooling. Despite its green technology, MeluXina delivers computing power equivalent to tens of thousands of PCs.

Goats to the rescue against wildfires in Spain

In Mataró, Catalonia, a pilot project is using around 300 goats to create natural firebreaks. The animals eat underbrush and thorny vegetation, reducing the risk of wildfires. Traditional livestock grazing is being revived, with shepherds receiving compensation for their work. The project is part of an EU-funded program addressing the growing wildfire threat in Southern Europe caused by climate change.

ESA satellite launched to improve weather forecasts

A new weather satellite has been launched from French Guiana. Metop-SGA1, developed by the European Space Agency (ESA), aims to improve short-term weather forecasts up to twelve days in advance. Earlier, MTG-S1 was also launched, primarily to enhance predictions of extreme weather events.

Hair-based toothpaste targets cavities

Scientists have developed a keratin-based toothpaste. It repairs tooth enamel, stops early cavities, and seals pain channels. The protein is derived from hair or wool and, together with minerals in saliva, forms a protective layer.

U.S. high-speed train for a transportation revolution

Starting at the end of August, new high-speed trains will connect Washington, New York, and Boston. The NextGen Acela trains offer more space, more frequent service, and aim to make switching from cars or planes easier. Built in the U.S., they have also secured 15,000 jobs.

After 86 years: Britain’s largest grasshopper returns

Thanks to the conservation program Hop of Hope, the Great Marsh Grasshopper has returned to the Broads. The species was last seen hatching in the wild in 1939, and its population had sharply declined due to habitat loss. Pre-treated eggs helped establish new populations in marshes and wetlands.

How technologies give people their voice back

New innovations are helping people with communication difficulties — from digitally preserved voices to robots and simple signage in public spaces. Whether it’s brain implants, “voice banking,” or playful tools, the goal is to enable expression and participation. Examples from the UK, Luxembourg and Italy show just how diverse these approaches can be.
